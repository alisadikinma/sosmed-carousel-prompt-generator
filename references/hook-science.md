Adapted from Sparkfluence Hook Library 2026, advertising psychology research, and virality scoring data.

## Golden Rule: Hook is NOT Just Text — It's Visual + Text + Emotion

A carousel hook must work on THREE layers simultaneously:
1. **Visual Hook** — The image itself stops the scroll (pattern interrupt in <0.5 seconds)
2. **Text Hook** — The headline creates a curiosity gap that MUST be scratched
3. **Emotional Hook** — The creator's expression triggers mirror neurons (shock, curiosity, excitement)

If ANY layer is weak, the hook fails.

## 5 Hook Categories (Ranked by Psychology)

### 1. VISUAL SHOCK & PATTERN INTERRUPT
**Psychology:** +400% dopamine spike for surprise (Copenhagen neuro studies)
**Best for:** Tech, Travel, Food, Showcase, High-Energy topics
**How it works:** Non-verbal visual cue stops scroll BEFORE the viewer reads any text.

**Carousel Application:**
- Extreme close-up on creator's face with exaggerated expression
- Split-screen comparison (before/after, good/bad)
- Unusual camera angle or perspective
- Visual contradiction (familiar object used in unexpected way)
- Bold color contrast against feed norm

**Hook Examples:**
- [Visual: Split screen comparison] "Mana yang lo pilih?"
- [Visual: Extreme close-up → zoom out] "Perhatiin baik-baik."
- [Visual: Unexpected scale comparison] "Segede ini ternyata."

### 2. NEGATIVE BIAS & WARNINGS
**Psychology:** Loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain motivation (Kahneman)
**Best for:** Education, Finance, Coding, Business, Health
**How it works:** Triggers "Am I doing this wrong?" fear. Negative superlatives get +63% higher CTR (Outbrain, 65K headlines).

**Carousel Application:**
- Warning-style headline with creator's shocked/concerned face
- "STOP doing X" format — implies viewer is making a mistake RIGHT NOW
- Negative framing of common behavior

**Hook Examples (Bahasa):**
- "STOP lakuin ini sekarang juga, keliatan amatir banget."
- "Kalau lo masih pake [Tool Lama] di 2026, lo udah ketinggalan."
- "Ini alasan kenapa [bisnis/project] lo bakal gagal dalam 6 bulan."
- "99% orang gagal di [Topik] sebelum mulai. Ini alasannya."
- "Lo udah salah lakuin [Topik] seumur hidup lo."

### 3. CURIOSITY GAP & INSIDER SECRETS
**Psychology:** Creates an "information itch" — open loop that MUST be closed
**Best for:** Storytelling, Vlogs, Case Studies, Motivation
**How it works:** Sephora testing shows blurred→reveal = +41% watch time, +27% engagement. NEVER reveal the payoff in the hook.

**Carousel Application:**
- Teaser headline that promises a reveal in later slides
- "I found a glitch" / "This feels illegal" framing
- Numbers that seem impossible (create "wait, really?" reaction)
- Creator expression = knowing smirk or raised eyebrows

**Hook Examples (Bahasa):**
- "Gue nemu celah di sistem [Industri]."
- "Ini mungkin 'hack' paling ilegal yang pernah gue temuin."
- "Gue coba [Hal Sulit] selama 30 hari dan hasilnya bikin takut."
- "Tool rahasia yang dipake [Orang Terkenal]."
- "Ini terasa ilegal untuk diketahui."

### 4. RELATABILITY & IDENTITY
**Psychology:** +91.7% engagement for "this is literally me" moments
**Best for:** Lifestyle, General Entertainment, Comedy
**How it works:** Viewer self-identifies with the scenario. Triggers "That's me!" or "I know someone exactly like this."

**Carousel Application:**
- POV format — "POV: Lo akhirnya [achieve something]"
- Direct address to specific audience — "Buat yang nonton jam 2 pagi..."
- Normalizing unpopular opinions
- Creator's "knowing look" expression (we've all been there)

**Hook Examples (Bahasa):**
- "POV: Lo akhirnya ngerti cara [Result] tanpa [Pain]."
- "Kalau lo [Job Title] dan gak tau ini, lo dalam bahaya."
- "Buat yang nonton ini jam 2 pagi..."
- "Kalau saldo rekening lo Rp 0, ini buat lo."
- "Bayangin kalau lo mulai [Topik] setahun lalu."

### 5. SPEED & VALUE PROMISE
**Psychology:** High perceived ROI on time invested
**Best for:** Tutorials, Quick Tips, Hacks
**How it works:** Promise massive value in minimal time. Creates instant commitment.

**Carousel Application:**
- "Give me 30 seconds" format
- "N steps" with explicit no-BS promise
- "Lazy person's guide" framing
- Creator's confident/authoritative expression

**Hook Examples (Bahasa):**
- "Kasih gue 30 detik dan gue hemat 30 jam kerja lo."
- "Cara [Result] dalam 3 langkah (tanpa basa-basi)."
- "Panduan orang males buat [Topik]."
- "Kuasai [Topik] dalam waktu kurang dari 60 detik."
- "Curi strategi gue buat [Result]."

## Carousel-Specific Hook Strategies

### The Triple Hook for Carousel Slide 1

Every Hook slide must work on 3 layers:

| Layer | Description | Prompt Implication |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Visual** | Creator's exaggerated expression + scene that contradicts expectation | Face expression + unusual scene composition in prompt |
| **Text** | Headline with curiosity gap (massive font, accent color on power word) | Bold text in gradient zone with power word highlighted |
| **Emotional** | Mirror neuron trigger — viewer FEELS what creator feels | Specific expression keywords in prompt (shock, disbelief, excitement) |

### Foreshadow Strategy (Slide 2)

> **Prompt implementation:** For the Foreshadow prompt template with 4 visual types, see `references/prompt-formulas.md` > "Template — Foreshadow Slide" section.

Slide 2 is CRITICAL because:
- Instagram re-serves carousel from slide 2 if user didn't engage on slide 1 (second chance algorithm)
- Slide 2 must create FOMO — "If you skip this, you'll miss [consequence]"

**Foreshadow Types:**
1. **Steps Tease** — "Ada 5 cara, dan yang ke-5 bikin gue berhenti kerja."
2. **Fear Urgency** — "Kalau lo skip ini, lo terus buang Rp 5jt/bulan tanpa sadar."
3. **Quiz Question** — "Mana yang lebih bagus — [A] atau [B]? Jawabannya bakal bikin kaget."
4. **Visual Tease** — Show blurred/partial preview of the reveal slide

### CTA Slide Hook (Last Slide)

> **Prompt implementation:** For the 4 CTA visual types with prompt templates, see `references/prompt-formulas.md` > "Template — CTA Slide" section.

The CTA must convert attention. Use these formulas:

| CTA Type | Formula | Example (Bahasa) |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| **Polarize** | Create debate with 2 sides | "Menurut lo [A] atau [B]? Tulis di komentar." |
| **Question** | Easy to answer, triggers curiosity | "Lo yang mana? Tulis 1, 2, atau 3!" |
| **Identity Tag** | Trigger share/tag behavior | "Tag temen yang BUTUH ini. Lo tau siapa." |
| **Engagement Reward** | Value exchange for engagement | "Comment 'GUIDE' dan gue DM step-by-step nya." |

**Key Stat:** First-person CTAs = +90% higher CTR. Single CTA focus = +371% clicks.

## Psychology Frameworks for Carousel Structure

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)
Best for: Educational, problem-aware audiences

```
Slide 1 (Hook): State the PROBLEM dramatically
Slide 2-3: AGITATE — make it worse, show consequences
Slide 4-7: SOLUTION — concrete steps, name tools
Slide 8+: PROOF — results, data
Last: CTA
```

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
Best for: Transformation, fitness, education

```
Slide 1 (Hook): Show the BEFORE (pain state)
Slide 2-3: Show the AFTER (desired state)
Slide 4-7: The BRIDGE — how to get from A to B
Last: CTA
```

### Hook-Story-Offer
Best for: TikTok, Reels, direct response

```
Slide 1 (Hook): Pattern interrupt + curiosity gap
Slide 2-7: STORY — narrative with progressive revelation
Slide 8+: OFFER — what they get for taking action
Last: CTA
```

## Emotional Arc for Maximum Engagement

> **Prompt implementation:** For the Emotional Arc Visual Map with beat→visual treatment mapping and intensity tags, see `references/prompt-formulas.md` > "Emotional Arc Visual Map" section.

The optimal carousel emotional arc follows a "roller coaster" pattern:

```
Slide 1: HIGH (shock/curiosity) ← Hook
Slide 2: DIP (problem/setup)
Slide 3-5: BUILD (progressive value, escalating stakes)
Slide 6-7: MINI-HOOK (surprise in middle — "but wait")
Slide 8+: CLIMAX (biggest reveal, most shareable insight)
Last: WARM (connection, CTA)
```

**Key insight:** Place a "mini-hook" or surprise at slides 5-7 to prevent the mid-carousel drop-off. Exit rates stabilize at 12-15% per slide after slide 4, but a mid-carousel surprise re-engages committed swipers.

## Cialdini's 6 Principles Applied to Carousels

| Principle | Carousel Application |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Reciprocity** | Give massive value in content slides → viewer feels compelled to follow/save |
| **Commitment** | "If you're someone who..." → viewer self-identifies → committed to swipe through |
| **Social Proof** | "Join 50,000+ people who..." → credibility via numbers |
| **Authority** | Creator face + professional setting → credibility transfer |
| **Liking** | "I was exactly where you are..." → relatability drives connection |
| **Scarcity** | "Only available this week" / "This info will be taken down" → urgency |

## Integration with Prompt Generation

When generating carousel prompts, the hook slide MUST include:

1. **Creator face** with EXAGGERATED expression matching the hook category:
   - Visual Shock → wide-eyed shock, mouth open
   - Negative Bias → concerned frown, warning gesture
   - Curiosity Gap → knowing smirk, raised eyebrows
   - Relatability → relatable frustration or joy
   - Speed & Value → confident authority, direct eye contact

2. **Headline** with minimum 3/5 scoring features (question, number, power word, negative frame, unrevealed payoff)

3. **Visual scene** that creates pattern interrupt (not generic background)

4. **Power word** in accent color within the headline

5. **Curiosity gap** — headline must create a question that can only be answered by swiping
